Fluorine

Facts

Element: Fluorine

Symbol: F

Atomic Mass: 18.998

Source: Mineral forms, like fluorite of fluorspar

State of matter at room temp: Gas

Chemical properties: most electronegative element at 3.8 and heat of vaporization at 6.51 kJ/mol

Oxidation number: -1, oxidizes oxygen

Physical Properties: cubic crystal structure and 64 pm covalent radius

Electron Configuration: [He]2s22p5

Number of Protons: 9

Number of Electrons: 9, at neutral state

Number of Neutrons: 10, at stable state

Price or Value: $5-8 per kg in a compound

Discovery Year: 1810

Discoverer: Andre-Marie Ampere

Group and Period Number: 17, 2 (respectively)

Common Isotope: 19F at 100%

Common Ionic Compounds: NaFl and VF6

Historic Relevance: Was used in 1529 for smelting

Uses before the 20th century: Fluorine salts for welding and frosting glass

Uses after the 20th century: Cable insulation, atomic bomb, and refrigerants
